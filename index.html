About

Dr. Mohammad Masum is an Assistant Professor in the Department of Applied Data Science at San José State University. His research focuses on applied artificial intelligence, large language models, multimodal learning, cybersecurity, healthcare analytics, and edge AI systems.

My research vision is to design AI systems that do not simply process data, but understand context, reason with evidence, and support reliable decision-making in high-impact domains.

Research Areas

Large Language Models

LLMs, RAG, instruction tuning, agentic AI, and reliable AI reasoning.

Multimodal AI

Vision-language models, recommendation systems, visual reasoning, and information management.

Cybersecurity AI

Malware detection, phishing analysis, intrusion detection, and trustworthy security intelligence.

Healthcare AI

Medical imaging, wearable sensors, clinical NLP, and non-invasive health monitoring.

Edge AI and TinyML

Efficient models for resource-constrained devices and real-time intelligent systems.

Featured Projects

VisioRAG

A multimodal recommendation framework combining vision transformers and retrieval-augmented generation to improve visual search and e-commerce information management.

InvadeAI

An interactive advertising system using multimodal AI to connect visual understanding, product context, and intelligent user engagement.

HybridSense-LLM

A structured multimodal framework for wellness prediction from wearable sensors and contextual self-reports.

TinyPulse+

A TinyML-based health monitoring project focused on non-invasive blood pressure monitoring and efficient deployment.

PE2Prompt

A large language model-native framework for interpretable static malware analysis.

LSE-Net

An integrated segmentation and ensemble deep learning framework for lung disease classification.
