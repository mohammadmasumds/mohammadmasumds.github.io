Multimodal Recommendation Systems

Vision Transformers • Large Language Models • RAG • Attribute-Aware Product Understanding

This research direction develops multimodal AI systems that improve product search, recommendation, and information management by combining visual understanding, language reasoning, and retrieval-augmented generation. The central goal is to move beyond keyword-based retrieval and image-only similarity toward systems that better understand customer intent, product attributes, and visual context.

Core Contributions

  • Integrates Vision Transformers and LLMs to improve e-commerce search and recommendation quality.
  • Uses LLM-generated captions to reduce the impact of noisy product descriptions and keyword stuffing.
  • Combines visual and textual representations through multimodal fusion for more context-aware retrieval.
  • Explores RAG-based query enhancement to better align recommendations with user intent.
  • Develops attribute-aware representation learning for fine-grained product understanding.

Representative Projects and Papers

BiLens: Multimodal Recommendation Framework

BiLens combines Vision Transformers and LLM-generated captions to improve product recommendation accuracy. Visual features are extracted using ViT, while image captions are transformed into contextual word embeddings. These representations are integrated through early fusion and compared using cosine similarity to retrieve products that better reflect customer intent.

VisioRAG: Vision Transformers and RAG for Recommendation

VisioRAG extends multimodal recommendation by using Retrieval-Augmented Generation and LLM-based caption enhancement. The framework uses Florence-2-large for image captioning, BERT for contextual embeddings, and Gemini for caption enhancement, enabling richer product representations and more human-like query understanding.

FACTOR: Constrained Representation Refinement

FACTOR addresses fine-grained attribute recognition in fashion and e-commerce by improving structured attribute prediction while preserving the stability of pretrained vision-language models. The framework focuses on attributes such as color, material, and pattern, which are essential for interpretable product understanding.

Impact

Together, these projects support the design of next-generation recommendation systems that understand products more like humans do: through visual details, semantic attributes, contextual descriptions, and user intent. This direction has applications in e-commerce, digital advertising, visual search, and intelligent information management.

Open-Vocabulary Product Understanding

Agentic AI • Vision-Language Models • Product Segmentation • Commercial Content Analysis

This research direction explores how multimodal AI systems can understand, localize, segment, and reason about products in unconstrained visual environments. The work focuses on open-vocabulary product discovery, agentic segmentation, synthetic data generation, and vision-language reasoning for large-scale commercial media analysis.

Research Themes

  • Open-vocabulary product discovery beyond fixed category taxonomies.
  • Agentic AI systems that coordinate multiple vision and language tools.
  • Synthetic segmentation dataset generation using diffusion models.
  • Evaluation of multimodal LLMs against specialized detection architectures.
  • Semantic verification for product-level reasoning in real-world visual media.

Representative Projects and Papers

CARVE: Open-Vocabulary Product Discovery and Segmentation

CARVE integrates Grounding DINO, SAM2, and CLIP-based semantic verification to support open-vocabulary product segmentation without task-specific retraining. The framework enables robust product discovery in real-world visual media and significantly improves segmentation quality and recall compared to closed-set baselines.

Agentic AI for Consumer Product Segmentation

This project investigates agentic segmentation frameworks that use Gemini 2.5 as a reasoning orchestrator coordinating specialized tools such as Grounding DINO and SAM2. The hybrid framework achieves strong segmentation quality while supporting flexible open-vocabulary reasoning.

Benchmarking Multimodal LLMs for Object Detection

This work provides a quantitative comparison between multimodal LLMs and specialized object detection models such as YOLO and DETR. The study evaluates prompting strategies, RAG, and LoRA fine-tuning to understand how general-purpose multimodal models compete in vision-centric tasks.

Prompt-Guided Synthetic Segmentation Generation

A diffusion-based inpainting framework designed to generate large-scale synthetic segmentation datasets using prompt-guided scene generation. The framework reduces dependence on manual annotation and improves segmentation performance in data-scarce settings.

Impact

Together, these projects contribute toward next-generation AI systems capable of understanding products in dynamic visual environments through multimodal reasoning, open-vocabulary perception, and agentic orchestration. Applications include e-commerce intelligence, visual search, social media analysis, digital advertising, and autonomous commercial content understanding.

Wearable AI and Multimodal Health Intelligence

Wearable Sensors • Large Language Models • Physiological Reasoning • Wellness Prediction

This research direction investigates how multimodal AI and large language models can support interpretable wellness prediction from wearable sensor data. The work focuses on combining physiological signals, statistical descriptors, contextual reasoning, and prompt-driven inference to build scalable and human-interpretable health intelligence systems.

Research Themes

  • LLM-based reasoning over physiological and behavioral signals.
  • Multimodal integration of waveform data and statistical health descriptors.
  • Prompt-engineering strategies for wellness prediction.
  • Interpretable AI for stress, fatigue, readiness, and sleep analysis.
  • Efficient and low-cost health intelligence systems for wearable devices.

Representative Projects and Papers

HybridSense-LLM

HybridSense-LLM is a structured multimodal framework that integrates wearable sensor signals, statistical feature extraction, and prompt-driven LLM reasoning for wellness prediction. The system evaluates multiple prompting strategies across OpenAI, Gemini, and DeepSeek models to estimate stress, fatigue, readiness, and sleep quality from wearable data.

Physiological Signal Reasoning with LLMs

This line of work explores how large language models can reason over structured physiological descriptors and waveform context to support transparent and interpretable health prediction. The research studies the interaction between statistical health features and natural-language reasoning strategies.

Prompt Engineering for Wellness Prediction

The framework systematically evaluates zero-shot, few-shot, self-consistency, and tree-of-thought prompting approaches for health-related prediction tasks. Results show that different prompting strategies provide task-dependent improvements across wellness indicators.

Impact

Together, these projects contribute toward next-generation health intelligence systems that are interpretable, scalable, and accessible through consumer wearable technologies. The research aims to bridge physiological sensing, multimodal AI, and language-model reasoning for practical real-world wellness monitoring.

Interpretable AI for Malware Analysis

Large Language Models • Static Malware Analysis • PE Features • Explainable Cybersecurity

This research direction develops interpretable and deployment-aware AI frameworks for static malware analysis. The work focuses on bridging structured Portable Executable (PE) features with large language model reasoning so that malware predictions are not only accurate, but also transparent, auditable, and useful for security analysts.

Research Themes

  • LLM-native malware classification from structured PE feature representations.
  • Feature-to-language transformation for interpretable cybersecurity reasoning.
  • Numerical feature summarization for byte histograms, entropy patterns, imports, exports, sections, and metadata.
  • Zero-shot and few-shot prompting for adaptable malware analysis without model retraining.
  • Multi-agent decomposition for feature-group-specific malware explanations and evidence attribution.

Representative Projects and Papers

PE2Prompt: LLM-Native Static Malware Analysis

PE2Prompt converts high-dimensional PE malware features into structured language-based prompts for large language model reasoning. The framework uses LLM-guided feature curation, numerical feature summarization, and structured prompt construction to support interpretable zero-shot and few-shot malware classification across models such as GPT-4o-mini, Claude, and DeepSeek.

Multi-Agent Malware Reasoning

This extension decomposes PE malware analysis into specialized agents aligned with EMBER feature groups, including sections, linkage, metadata, histogram, byte entropy, and printable strings. Each agent produces an independent verdict and evidence-grounded explanation, which are aggregated through weighted voting and summarized into a final analyst-facing rationale.

Feature-to-Language Cybersecurity Reasoning

This line of work formalizes a new feature-to-language paradigm for cybersecurity, where numerical and structural malware indicators are transformed into natural language reasoning templates. The goal is to make static malware workflows more explainable, adaptable, and easier to audit in real-world security settings.

Impact

These projects aim to advance practical and explainable malware detection by combining structured static analysis with language-model reasoning. The broader goal is to support cybersecurity analysts with systems that can classify suspicious files, identify evidence behind predictions, and provide transparent rationales without requiring expensive retraining for every new deployment context.
